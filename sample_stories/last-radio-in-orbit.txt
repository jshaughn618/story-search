The station had drifted silent except for one ancient shortwave set bolted near the cupola.

Commander Imani spun its dial every morning, hoping to catch a weather report from Earth, or music, or static that sounded human.

On day 412, the radio answered with her own voice from sixteen years earlier, reciting her pilot oath in a trembling academy auditorium.

The transmission repeated once a day, one year at a time, moving forward through her life. Graduation. Her first launch. Her mother's funeral.

When it reached the present, the signal went quiet. Then a new voice broke in: "Ground to Imani. We have your coordinates. Hold your orbit."
